On Grading in Higher Education


I am well-aware of the fact that I am not the first person, and especially not the first student, to find fault with the grading system. The extent of the issues it causes, however, were not fully clear to me until I began working as an Instructional Assistant at the University of Michigan. At this school, we often have large lecture sections and accompanying smaller weekly sections for discussion. These periods are supposed to be opportunities for us to break down concepts and interact with content a little more deeply. Ask questions that we can't in a 200 person lecture. Ideally, this is the think-tank where we represent the idea of higher education and a general scholarly thirst for knowledge and exploration.

As my prose clearly alluded to, this is unfortunately not the case at all.
